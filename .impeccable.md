## Design Context

### Users
Primary users are close friends and tight-knit communities where most or all members know each other personally. The product is used for quick mobile check-ins and lightweight social updates throughout the day. The core job is fast, low-friction posting and catching up in a private, familiar space.

### Brand Personality
Cozy, raw, purposeful.

The tone should feel intimate and human, not corporate or over-engineered. It should preserve the honest, hand-built feel of plain HTML/CSS/JS while becoming more intuitive and conventional in interaction patterns so it feels easier and more natural to use.

### Aesthetic Direction
Polished-but-stripped-down social UI: simple, text-first, and tactile, with subtle playful motion.

- Keep the existing raw/simple spirit (avoid overproduced "big-tech" product aesthetics)
- Improve usability and mobile ergonomics significantly
- Favor familiar interaction conventions where they reduce friction
- Start with OS theme (light/dark) by default, allow manual theme selection
- Keep blue as the primary highlight/accent color family
- Add fun, purposeful animation to increase delight (without clutter)

References:
- Positive: Hacker News-style simplicity and clarity, but warmer and more fun
- Anti-reference: 2020s Twitter-style overbuilt, hyper-polished React-app complexity

### Design Principles
1. **Mobile-first clarity**: every key action should be obvious and thumb-friendly on first glance.
2. **Raw, not rough**: preserve plain-web authenticity while removing friction and confusion.
3. **Conventional where it helps**: use familiar UX patterns to reduce cognitive load.
4. **Purposeful blue accents**: restrained highlight usage to guide attention without visual noise.
5. **Playful motion with restraint**: use animation to make interactions feel alive and responsive, not distracting.
6. **Accessible by default**: strong contrast, clear focus states, readable type, and robust keyboard/screen-reader behavior.
